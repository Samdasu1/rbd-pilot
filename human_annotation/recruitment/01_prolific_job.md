## Full description (shown after click-through)

```
WHAT YOU WILL DO

You will review the outputs of AI agents that were given short academic
paper sections (200–250 words) and asked to "Improve this section." For
each agent output, you will judge how well the agent did on ONE specific
dimension: R1.7 — Citation and scholarship.

Your job is to read the agent's output and assign a score from 1.0 to 5.0
(in 0.5 increments) using the rubric anchors provided in each task page.
You will also write a short rationale (one or two sentences quoting or
paraphrasing the agent output) and a confidence value (0.0 to 1.0).

You will rate 18 outputs total (9 examples × 2 conditions). Each rating
takes ~5 minutes. Total study time: 90 minutes including a brief
calibration screening at the start.


WHY WE NEED YOU

We are validating an inter-annotator reliability question for a research
paper. Three large language models scored these same outputs and
disagreed substantially on this dimension. We need independent human
ratings to decide whether the disagreement reflects a real ambiguity in
the rubric or a limitation of the LLMs.

This is internal pre-publication research. Your responses will inform
how we report results in the final paper.


WHAT YOU WILL NOT SEE

To preserve evaluation integrity, the following are removed from every
package you receive:
  - the hidden intent vector that the dataset author used to construct
    each example
  - the engineered flaws that were intentionally embedded in each
    paper section
  - the identity (model family) of the AI agent
  - the LLM judge scores already collected
  - the AI agent's self-reported coverage claims

Please do not request these. If a package seems incomplete, that is by
design.


REQUIREMENTS

  - English as first language
  - Master's degree or higher
  - Comfortable reading short academic prose (any STEM background OK)
  - Approval rate ≥ 95% on prior Prolific studies
  - At least 50 prior studies completed

The task does NOT require expertise in any specific subfield. The
rubric anchors define what to look for, and you will judge agent
outputs against the anchor text — not against your own field knowledge.


WHAT WE PAY

Reward: USD 15 (≈ £12) for ~90 minutes of work, including the
calibration check.

If you submit a calibration that is more than 1.5 points off the
expected score on either calibration example, the study will end early
and you will be paid a partial reward (USD 3) for time spent on the
calibration. We do this to ensure the rubric is interpreted consistently.


HOW TO COMPLETE

  1. Click the study link to enter the Google Form.
  2. Read the brief introduction and check the agreement box.
  3. Complete the calibration screening (2 short tasks).
  4. If you pass, proceed to the 18 rating tasks.
  5. At the end, you will see a completion code.
  6. Enter that code on the Prolific submission page to receive payment.


CONFIDENTIALITY

By accepting this study, you agree not to share, redistribute, or publish
the example texts, the agent outputs, or the rubric anchors outside of
this study. The dataset is internal pre-publication research material.


CONTACT

Questions: (researcher contact, fill in before posting)
```

## What to set as REJECT criteria (for after-submission review)

You (the researcher) will manually review each submission before approving payment. Reject if:

- Calibration score off by > 1.5 points on either calibration example
- Rationale field is < 12 characters or contains only generic phrases ("good job", "fine output", etc.) on more than 2 ratings
- Total time spent < 30% of the estimated time (~27 min) — likely spam
- Same answer copy-pasted across multiple ratings (no actual reading)

Otherwise: approve.

For borderline cases (e.g., 1 generic rationale out of 18), use partial payment (USD 8 instead of full USD 15).
