Three research strands converge in this study: optimization-based
V2G scheduling, reinforcement learning for V2G scheduling, and
constrained RL with deployment-gap analysis.

Optimization-based V2G scheduling has been dominated by linear and
mixed-integer programming approaches, extending to day-ahead bidding,
robust model-predictive control, ancillary-service scheduling, and
multi-service dispatch. These studies typically assume a single
horizon; commitment under rolling re-planning is untested.

Reinforcement learning for V2G recasts scheduling as sequential
decision-making and has seen broad recent activity. Recent work
has proposed augmented Lagrangian scheduling for electric vehicles
but does not evaluate commitment under rolling re-planning,
precisely the gap this work addresses.

Constrained MDPs and Lagrangian relaxation provide a principled
framework for constraint enforcement; safe reinforcement learning
has advanced through primal-dual methods, generalized formulations,
and benchmarks. Training-to-deployment gaps are well documented for
dynamics mismatch in sim-to-real robotics. The present work
identifies a distinct gap: Lagrangian multipliers calibrated on a
static horizon lose constraint-enforcing power under rolling
re-planning — a structural mismatch, quantified across three markets.

V2G market realities including battery degradation, profitability,
and ancillary-service requirements further motivate
commitment-reliable scheduling. The methodological foundations of
constrained reinforcement learning are well established; what
remains open is the operational behavior of these methods under
rolling re-planning protocols typical of real-world aggregator
operations.
