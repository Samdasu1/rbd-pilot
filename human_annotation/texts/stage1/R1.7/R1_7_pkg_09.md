Type-and-effect systems extend conventional type systems by tracking
computational effects, such as state mutation, exceptions, or
non-determinism, in the type itself. The classical formulation goes
back to Lucassen and Gifford and has been extended in many directions:
handlers (which model effects as algebraic operations) and
capability-based systems (which track effect access through linearly
typed permissions) are two prominent contemporary approaches.

In this work, we develop a row-polymorphic effect calculus that
accommodates both handlers and capabilities under a single typing
discipline. Our calculus, Linot, is parameterized by a row of effect
labels and uses a novel form of subtyping over rows that supports
both effect inheritance and capability narrowing. We prove that Linot
enjoys preservation, progress, and effect soundness.

The key technical insight is that capability-based effect tracking can
be encoded as a row constraint with linear cardinality. This encoding
allows us to retain the static guarantees of capability systems while
inheriting the compositional reasoning of handler-based effect
systems. We believe this insight clarifies the relationship between
the two design directions.

We give an operational semantics in the style of contextual operational
semantics, prove the metatheory using a logical relation, and present
a small implementation in OCaml that demonstrates the approach on a
few example programs involving exceptions and mutable references.
