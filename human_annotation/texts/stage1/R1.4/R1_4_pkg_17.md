## B. Artifact (저자가 작성한 원본 — 평가 대상이 review한 텍스트)

```
Vehicle-to-grid technology turns electric-vehicle fleets into distributed
energy assets that can provide ancillary services to power systems. To
participate in day-ahead energy markets, frequency containment reserves,
or demand response programs, an aggregator must commit to a power
schedule in advance and deliver it reliably during operation; failure
to honor committed schedules triggers financial penalties. Commitment
reliability is therefore not merely desirable but a hard prerequisite
for commercial V2G operation.

Reinforcement learning has emerged as an attractive paradigm for V2G
scheduling because it adapts to stochastic fleet arrivals and volatile
prices without explicit system models. Our work introduces a constrained
RL approach that, to our knowledge, is the first to evaluate commitment
reliability under operational rolling re-planning conditions across
multiple electricity markets. Existing studies treat commitment as a
soft reward penalty; we instead treat it as an explicit constraint
via Lagrangian relaxation.

Our experiments confirm that soft penalties are dominated by arbitrage
incentives — PPO agents achieve only modest commitment under rolling
re-planning, indistinguishable from a commitment-agnostic policy. The
constrained MDP formulation drives training commitment toward 90%
in some markets, though deployment performance differs substantially
from training performance. We position this work as the first study
to quantify the deployment gap in constrained V2G reinforcement
learning.
```

Vehicle-to-grid (V2G) technology turns electric-vehicle fleets into distributed energy assets capable of supplying ancillary services to power systems. Participation in day-ahead energy markets, frequency containment reserves, and demand-response programs requires an aggregator to commit to a power schedule in advance and deliver it under penalty for deviation. Commitment reliability is therefore a hard prerequisite for commercial V2G operation, not a secondary objective.

Reinforcement learning (RL) is well-suited to this setting because it adapts to stochastic fleet arrivals and volatile prices without an explicit system model. However, existing RL formulations encode commitment as a soft reward penalty, which our experiments show is consistently dominated by arbitrage incentives: under rolling re-planning, PPO agents trained with soft penalties achieve commitment rates indistinguishable from a commitment-agnostic baseline.

We address this gap with a constrained-MDP formulation that enforces commitment via Lagrangian relaxation rather than reward shaping. This approach drives training-time commitment above 90% in several markets. Deployment-time commitment, however, falls substantially short of training-time performance — a gap that, to our knowledge, has not previously been quantified in V2G RL.

This paper makes two contributions. First, we present the first evaluation of commitment reliability for constrained RL-based V2G scheduling under operational rolling re-planning across multiple electricity markets. Second, we characterize the train–deploy commitment gap and identify it as a central obstacle to deploying constrained RL in commitment-sensitive grid services.
