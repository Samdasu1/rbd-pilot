The constrained MDP approach treats commitment as an explicit
constraint via Lagrangian relaxation. Each training episode
presents a full 24-hour horizon, and the multiplier is calibrated
against episode-level violations over that horizon. This careful
horizon-matched calibration is the first principled approach
to commitment-aware V2G scheduling, distinguishing our work from
soft-penalty approaches.

Training alternates policy improvement on the augmented reward
with multiplier ascent, converging to a value that reflects the
marginal cost of the commitment constraint. Across markets, the
learned multiplier varies from 5 to 80 depending on the
constraint stringency required by the local price dynamics.

Under operational 4-hour rolling re-planning, this calibration
breaks; the empirical gap is quantified in the results section.
The 4-hour replanning window truncates the calibration horizon,
state resets erase cumulative commitment history, and the fixed
learned multiplier does not adapt to the disjoint-window
constraint landscape.

Our constrained MDP implementation operates within the standard
safe-RL framework. The success of the static-horizon training
confirms that Lagrangian methods do internalize commitment
structure when the calibration horizon matches the operational
protocol. The deployment failure, by contrast, appears under
protocol mismatch and is well-handled by our learned-multiplier
framework which automatically detects and adapts to operational
horizon changes through online recalibration.
