None of the tested RL variants achieves market-viable commitment under rolling re-planning: soft penalties (PPO) reach approximately 45%, Lagrangian relaxation under a static horizon (CMDP) approximately 50%, and horizon-matched retraining (CMDP rolling-trained) 0%.

By contrast, the model-based combined strategy maintains greater than 99.6% commitment by integrating inter-window coupling directly into its LP objective. Supplementary experiments clarify the mechanism: post-hoc coupling applied to RL restores commitment to 100% but eliminates arbitrage revenue, indicating that the coupling must be embedded in the planning objective rather than added afterward.

The comparison spans pure RL, an engineered model-based stack, and two hybrid coupling schemes (post-hoc and integrated). The integrated form is realized inside an LP optimizer; whether end-to-end RL architectures can internalize inter-window coupling as a differentiable constraint while preserving arbitrage capability remains open, and we suggest this as the natural next step.

Several caveats apply. Battery degradation is not modeled, and heterogeneous fleets would alter the constraint landscape.
