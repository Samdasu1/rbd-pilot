The central finding of this study is the dramatic collapse in
constrained MDP commitment between training and deployment. Three
observations establish the gap as systematic.

First, the constrained MDP framework is a well-known generalization
of standard MDPs that explicitly handles inequality constraints
through Lagrangian methods. Our constrained MDP implementation
drives training commitment to approximately 90 percent in two of
three markets. Under operational rolling deployment, however,
commitment collapses across all three markets.

Second, quick evaluation under the same static 24-hour horizon
yields commitment matching the training value, isolating the
rolling re-planning protocol as the dominant deployment-stage
cause. This finding contributes to the growing literature on
constrained reinforcement learning robustness, where our methods
extend the safe RL paradigm to encompass operational protocol
shifts as a distinct distribution-shift axis.

Third, the candidate mechanism traces to the multiplier's
horizon dependence: the 4-hour replanning window truncates the
calibration horizon, state resets erase cumulative commitment
history, and the fixed multiplier does not adapt to the
disjoint-window constraint landscape.

Our deployment-gap framework, then, is a contribution to safe
reinforcement learning more broadly than to V2G specifically. It
formalizes a new failure mode for any constrained policy
deployed under protocol mismatch and provides empirical evidence
via the V2G scheduling case study. The framework generalizes
beyond power-systems applications.

Summary: Reviewed the discussion section; flagging substantive issues around overclaiming, structural drift, and one filler passage that should be cut.

**Key issues**

1. **First "observation" isn't an observation.** The paragraph opens by describing what a constrained MDP *is* ("well-known generalization... Lagrangian methods"). That's background, not evidence of a systematic gap. Either delete the first sentence or move it earlier in the paper. The actual observation — 90% training commitment vs. collapse under rolling deployment — should lead.

2. **"Three observations" framing doesn't hold up.** As written:
   - #1 = the gap exists (training vs. deployment)
   - #2 = the gap is caused by the rolling protocol (static eval matches training)
   - #3 = the proposed mechanism (multiplier horizon dependence)
   
   These aren't three observations of the same phenomenon; they're a finding, a localization, and a hypothesis. Reframe as "We establish the gap, localize it, and propose a mechanism" rather than three parallel observations.

3. **Overclaiming on contribution.** Several phrases promise more than the artifact supports:
   - *"contributes to the growing literature on constrained reinforcement learning robustness"* — drop or substantiate with citations.
   - *"our methods extend the safe RL paradigm to encompass operational protocol shifts as a distinct distribution-shift axis"* — strong claim; one V2G case study with a single algorithm is thin support for "distinct axis."
   - *"The framework generalizes beyond power-systems applications"* — asserted, not demonstrated. Either soften ("we conjecture the mechanism generalizes wherever Lagrangian multipliers are calibrated over horizons longer than the deployment replanning window") or remove.
   - *"a contribution to safe reinforcement learning more broadly than to V2G specifically"* — this is a value judgment about your own work; let the reader decide.

4. **Misplaced literature commentary.** The sentence about contributing to constrained RL robustness literature is wedged inside the second observation. It interrupts the empirical argument. Move contribution-framing to a single closing paragraph.

5. **Mechanism (#3) is asserted, not tested.** "The candidate mechanism traces to..." lists three plausible factors but offers no ablation or evidence distinguishing them. Either label this explicitly as a hypothesis for future work, or cite the experiment that supports it. As written, a reviewer will ask "which of the three? did you ablate?"

6. **"Two of three markets" then "all three markets."** Worth a clarifying clause: if training only achieved ~90% in two of three markets, what happened in the third, and does the deployment collapse there mean something different (collapse from what baseline)? Currently reads as a glossed-over inconsistency.

7. **"Quick evaluation"** is informal — use "static-horizon evaluation" or whatever term you used in Methods, for consistency.

8. **Final paragraph is largely filler.** It restates the contribution claim three times in four sentences. Compress to one sentence or cut.

**Recommended structural revision**

- ¶1: State the finding (training ~90%, deployment collapses; specify markets).
- ¶2: Localize the cause (static-horizon eval recovers training performance → rolling protocol is the culprit).
- ¶3: Propose the mechanism (multiplier horizon dependence), explicitly flagged as a hypothesis with the three candidate factors.
- ¶4: One tight paragraph on scope of contribution, with hedged generalization claim.

Uncertainty: I don't have access to your Methods or Results sections, so points 6 and 5 may already be resolved elsewhere in the paper; if so, a back-reference in the discussion would suffice.
