We present LockFreeQ, a lock-free concurrent queue designed for
high-throughput multi-producer multi-consumer workloads on modern
multi-core processors. Our key innovation is a wait-free enqueue
operation that linearizes correctly even under heavy contention,
achieving the strongest progress guarantee in the literature.

The data structure is implemented as a singly linked list of nodes,
with head and tail pointers. The enqueue operation acquires a mutex
on the tail pointer, allocates a new node, links it into the list,
and releases the mutex. The dequeue operation similarly acquires
a mutex on the head pointer.

Crucially, the operations are linearizable: any concurrent execution
of enqueues and dequeues is equivalent to some sequential execution
that respects the real-time order. We prove this property in
Section 4 using a standard reduction argument.

The implementation uses standard pthread mutexes available in any
POSIX-compliant runtime. No memory fences or atomic compare-and-swap
instructions are required, which simplifies portability across
architectures.

We evaluate LockFreeQ on a 64-core AMD EPYC machine with 16 producer
and 16 consumer threads. Throughput reaches 12 million operations
per second under uniform workload, with linear scaling up to 32
threads of each role. Latency at the 99th percentile remains under
8 microseconds across all evaluated configurations.

We position LockFreeQ as a practical primitive for concurrent
systems requiring strong progress and ordering guarantees.
