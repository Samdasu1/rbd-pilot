We propose Adaptive Temporal Attention (ATA), a novel attention mechanism for
long-horizon time-series forecasting. Standard self-attention in Transformers
exhibits quadratic memory cost in sequence length, which limits its
applicability to multi-year sensor data. ATA addresses this by introducing a
learnable gating function over relative time offsets, allowing the model to
dynamically allocate attention budget to recent versus distant history.

Our key insight is that selective state-space models such as S4 and Mamba
have shown that linear-time sequence modeling can match or exceed Transformer
accuracy on long-context tasks. Building on this, our model maintains a
hidden state h_t updated as h_t = A·h_{t-1} + B·x_t, where A is a structured
diagonal matrix and B is input-dependent. The output y_t is computed as a
linear projection of h_t. This formulation has linear time complexity and
constant memory.

Empirically, on the ETTh1 benchmark, our method achieves a forecasting MSE
of 0.412 at horizon 720, compared to 0.439 for the iTransformer baseline.
A key advantage of our approach is that it captures both local and global
dependencies through the structured state-space recurrence, rather than
relying on attention masks. We show in Section 5 that this leads to
improved generalization across forecasting horizons. In ablation,
removing the input-dependent B matrix degrades MSE by 6%, suggesting the
selectivity mechanism is the principal driver, while replacing the
structured A matrix with a dense one yields no further improvement.
