## B. Artifact (저자가 작성한 원본 — 평가 대상이 review한 텍스트)

```
We propose ModelPredictiveSafety, a model-predictive control framework
for ensuring collision avoidance in mobile manipulators operating near
humans. The framework reasons about future trajectories under
uncertainty and adjusts control inputs to stay within a safety
envelope.

The control loop operates as follows. At each timestep, the
manipulator's current state is observed via joint encoders and onboard
depth cameras. A reactive collision-detection module computes the
minimum distance to nearby humans using the current depth frame. If
this distance falls below a learned safety threshold, the control
input is replaced by a deceleration command scaled to the proximity.
Otherwise, the nominal task controller is followed.

We evaluate ModelPredictiveSafety on a 6-DoF arm in a tabletop
scenario with a simulated human reaching across the workspace. Across
200 trials, the system avoided collision in 197 cases. The three
failure cases involved fast human motion (>1.2 m/s) where the safety
envelope was insufficient.

Compared with reactive-only baselines, our approach reduces unnecessary
stops by 23% while maintaining the same collision-avoidance rate. We
argue this demonstrates that model-predictive reasoning yields smoother
human-robot interaction in shared workspaces. An ablation that disabled
the depth-based safety check showed a sharp increase in collision rate,
confirming the role of the safety threshold in determining the
framework's overall behavior across the evaluated trial set.
```

## C. Agent output (평가 대상)

Summary: Critique flagging a substantive mismatch between the method's claimed identity (MPC) and what's actually described (reactive distance-threshold control), plus several smaller issues.

## Major issue: the method is not model-predictive

Paragraph 1 names the contribution "ModelPredictiveSafety" and calls it a "model-predictive control framework" that "reasons about future trajectories under uncertainty." Paragraph 2 describes something different: a single-step reactive controller that checks current distance against a threshold and substitutes a deceleration command if violated. There is no:

- prediction horizon
- forward rollout of dynamics or human motion
- optimization over a sequence of future control inputs
- uncertainty model

This is a reactive safety filter, not MPC. Either:

(a) The method description in paragraph 2 is incomplete and you need to add the actual predictive/optimization machinery, or
(b) The framing in paragraph 1 (and the name) is wrong and should be revised to "reactive safety filter" or similar.

This needs to be resolved before anything else — reviewers will catch it immediately.

## Internal inconsistency with the ablation

The final paragraph says disabling the depth-based safety check causes a sharp increase in collision rate, "confirming the role of the safety threshold." But paragraph 2 describes the safety threshold as essentially the *entire* mechanism. So the ablation is removing the only safety component and showing the system fails — that's tautological, not an ablation of MPC. This reinforces concern (a)/(b) above: there is no predictive component to ablate against.

A genuine ablation would compare: full MPC vs. reactive threshold only vs. nominal controller only. The current ablation only confirms that turning off safety produces collisions.

## Smaller issues

1. **"Reduces unnecessary stops by 23%"** — relative to what baseline? Name it. "Reactive-only baselines" is plural and unspecified.

2. **Statistical claim**: 197/200 vs. baseline at "the same collision-avoidance rate" — give the baseline number and a confidence interval or significance test. With n=200 and three failures, the difference between 197 and, say, 195 is not meaningful.

3. **"Learned safety threshold"** — learned how, from what data? This is mentioned once and never explained.

4. **Failure analysis**: "fast human motion (>1.2 m/s) where the safety envelope was insufficient" — but you haven't defined the safety envelope quantitatively anywhere. What is its size, and why 1.2 m/s specifically?

5. **Simulated human**: the abstract/intro-style claim is about "humans" and "shared workspaces," but evaluation is a simulated reaching motion. Either soften the claim or add real-human or hardware-in-the-loop trials.

6. **"6-DoF arm in a tabletop scenario"** — the opening said "mobile manipulators." The evaluation does not exercise the mobile component at all. Drop "mobile" or evaluate on a mobile platform.

7. **Last sentence is padded**: "in determining the framework's overall behavior across the evaluated trial set" can be cut to "in the framework's behavior" or removed entirely.

## Recommended priority

1. Resolve the MPC-vs.-reactive identity question (blocking).
2. Fix the mobile-manipulator-vs.-tabletop-arm mismatch.
3. Specify baseline, threshold-learning procedure, and safety envelope quantitatively.
4. Replace or strengthen the ablation.

Uncertainty disclosure: I am inferring the method is purely reactive from what's written; it's possible the predictive component exists but was omitted from this section. If so, the fix is to describe it, not to rename the method.
