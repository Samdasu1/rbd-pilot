This paper quantifies a substantial commitment collapse in constrained reinforcement learning for V2G fleet aggregation across three European markets. Under rolling re-planning, none of the RL variants achieves market-viable commitment: soft penalties (PPO) reach ~45%, Lagrangian relaxation on a static horizon (CMDP) ~50%, and horizon-matched retraining (CMDP rolling-trained) 0%.

By contrast, the model-based combined strategy maintains >99.6% commitment by integrating inter-window coupling directly into its LP objective. Supplementary experiments show that post-hoc coupling applied to RL restores commitment to 100% but eliminates arbitrage revenue, indicating that coupling must enter the planning objective itself rather than be imposed after the fact.

Our comparison spans pure RL, an engineered model-based stack, and two hybrid coupling schemes (post-hoc and integrated), where the integrated form is realized within an LP optimizer. Whether end-to-end RL architectures can internalize inter-window coupling as a differentiable constraint while preserving arbitrage capability remains open.

Several caveats apply. Historical day-ahead prices are replayed, but fleet dynamics are simulated, so absolute cost figures should be read as relative rather than deployment-grade projections.
