We introduce QMaster, a value-based reinforcement learning algorithm
designed for continuous-action robotic control tasks. Our key insight
is that explicit estimation of the action-value function permits
more sample-efficient learning than approaches that only model the
policy implicitly.

The QMaster training procedure operates as follows. At each iteration,
the agent collects trajectories using the current policy. The
trajectories are stored in a replay buffer of fixed capacity. From
the buffer, we sample minibatches and perform gradient steps on the
policy parameters to maximize the expected return.

Specifically, we use the standard policy gradient estimator with a
learned baseline. The policy is parameterized as a Gaussian over
actions with state-dependent mean and a fixed diagonal covariance.
Updates apply the REINFORCE estimator with the baseline subtracted
to reduce variance. We use Adam optimization with learning rate 3e-4
and apply gradient clipping at norm 5.

We evaluate QMaster on three continuous-control tasks from the MuJoCo
suite: HalfCheetah, Hopper, and Walker2d. After 10 million environment
steps, QMaster reaches expert-level performance on HalfCheetah and
Hopper, and competitive performance on Walker2d.

Compared to standard policy-gradient baselines, QMaster achieves
superior sample efficiency in the early stages of training. We argue
this demonstrates the practical advantage of value-based reasoning
in continuous-action settings.
