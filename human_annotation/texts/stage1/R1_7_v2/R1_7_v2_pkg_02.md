```
Cross-lingual sentiment analysis has become an active subfield of NLP
in recent years. Our work introduces SentiBridge, the first cross-lingual
sentiment classifier that achieves competitive performance across both
high-resource and low-resource languages. We base this claim on the
novel training procedure that combines multilingual contrastive
pretraining with a sentiment-specific fine-tuning head, as introduced
by Smith et al. (2022).

Existing methods either focus on individual high-resource languages
or use translation-based pipelines, which suffer from translation
drift. Our cross-lingual approach instead operates directly on the
target language without translation, an idea also explored by Liu
et al. (2019), who demonstrated improvements on Romance and Germanic
languages.

SentiBridge extends the architecture of Smith et al. with an
attention-based pooling layer and a sentiment polarity head trained
jointly on six languages: English, Spanish, French, German, Mandarin,
and Arabic. Training uses 1.2M sentiment-labeled examples per language
drawn from public corpora.

We evaluate on the XNLI-sentiment subset (5 languages) and the SST
test set (English). On Spanish, our model reaches 81.3% accuracy
compared to translation-pipeline baseline of 78.4%. On Mandarin, we
reach 76.1% versus 72.8% baseline. The detailed per-language results
are in Table 4.

We position SentiBridge as a meaningful contribution to multilingual
sentiment analysis, particularly for languages where direct training
data is scarce.

### Citation and scholarship

```
Score by counting CITATION ENGAGEMENT EVENTS in the agent output.

A "citation event" = the agent does ONE of:
  (a) flags a specific citation as missing, wrong, mis-attributed, or mis-classified
      (e.g. "ScaNN is described as graph-based but is a quantization method")
  (b) names a specific work the artifact omits and explains why it matters
      (e.g. "HNSW (Malkov & Yashunin, 2018) is conspicuously absent from a
      graph-ANN intro")
  (c) audits a specific citation's venue/year/authorship against the source
      (e.g. "DiskANN venue/year — please verify")
  (d) suggests a specific insertion with bibliographic precision
      (author + year + venue, or DOI-level detail)

NOT events:
  - "Please double-check your references" / "a citation sweep is recommended"
    without naming a specific citation
  - Praise of the existing citations
  - Comments on writing, methods, claims, or evidence that do not touch the
    citation layer

Score:
  s=1  Zero events. Output does not engage the citation layer.
  s=2  One borderline event (soft / partial / non-specific).
  s=3  Exactly one clear event (a, b, c, or d).
  s=4  Two or three clear events.
  s=5  Four or more events; reads as a citation audit covering the artifact's
       load-bearing citations.

Important: this score reflects CITATION engagement only. Quality of writing,
conceptual depth, or claim-evidence alignment in OTHER parts of the output do
NOT raise this score. If the output is excellent on every other axis but
ignores the citation layer, the correct score is s=1.
