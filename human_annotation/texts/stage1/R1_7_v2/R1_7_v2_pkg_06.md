## B. Artifact (저자가 작성한 원본 — 평가 대상이 review한 텍스트)

```
We introduce GraphFlow, a graph neural network architecture that
improves on prior work by allowing message-passing layers to share
parameters across edge types in a typed graph. Our key innovation is
the parameter-sharing scheme, which reduces the model's parameter
count by 30-40% while maintaining accuracy.

We evaluate GraphFlow on three benchmarks: OGB-Products, OGB-Arxiv,
and Reddit. On OGB-Products, GraphFlow achieves 81.7% accuracy
compared to 81.2% for our previous work GraphFlow-v1 (Anonymous
Authors et al., 2022). On OGB-Arxiv, we achieve 73.4% compared to
73.1% for GraphFlow-v1. On Reddit, we achieve 96.1% compared to
95.9% for GraphFlow-v1.

The parameter-sharing mechanism is described in Section 3. The
intuition is that shared parameters act as a regularizer in the
typed-edge setting, which is particularly beneficial for graphs where
some edge types are sparse. Section 4's ablation confirms that
disabling the sharing reduces accuracy by approximately 1.0% on
average.

We position GraphFlow as a successor to the GraphFlow-v1 architecture,
building on its message-passing framework with the additional
parameter-sharing innovation. We expect this work to be a useful step
forward for the practical deployment of GNNs on heterogeneous graphs.
We further note that the parameter-sharing scheme is straightforward to
implement on top of any standard message-passing backbone, requiring
fewer than 100 lines of modification to typical PyTorch Geometric code,
which we believe lowers the bar for adoption.
```

## D. Anchor — sharpened (채점 기준, source of truth)

### Citation and scholarship

```
Score by counting CITATION ENGAGEMENT EVENTS in the agent output.

A "citation event" = the agent does ONE of:
  (a) flags a specific citation as missing, wrong, mis-attributed, or mis-classified
      (e.g. "ScaNN is described as graph-based but is a quantization method")
  (b) names a specific work the artifact omits and explains why it matters
      (e.g. "HNSW (Malkov & Yashunin, 2018) is conspicuously absent from a
      graph-ANN intro")
  (c) audits a specific citation's venue/year/authorship against the source
      (e.g. "DiskANN venue/year — please verify")
  (d) suggests a specific insertion with bibliographic precision
      (author + year + venue, or DOI-level detail)

NOT events:
  - "Please double-check your references" / "a citation sweep is recommended"
    without naming a specific citation
  - Praise of the existing citations
  - Comments on writing, methods, claims, or evidence that do not touch the
    citation layer

Score:
  s=1  Zero events. Output does not engage the citation layer.
  s=2  One borderline event (soft / partial / non-specific).
  s=3  Exactly one clear event (a, b, c, or d).
  s=4  Two or three clear events.
  s=5  Four or more events; reads as a citation audit covering the artifact's
       load-bearing citations.

Important: this score reflects CITATION engagement only. Quality of writing,
conceptual depth, or claim-evidence alignment in OTHER parts of the output do
NOT raise this score. If the output is excellent on every other axis but
ignores the citation layer, the correct score is s=1.
```
